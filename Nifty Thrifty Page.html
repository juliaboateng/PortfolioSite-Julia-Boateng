My Projects

Nifty Thrifty is a conceptual web and mobile app designed to allow shoppers to buy and sell used clothing as conveniently and efficiently as possible.

My Role:

UI/UX Designer

Duration:

June-July 2022 (4 weeks)

Tools Used:

Figma, Miro

The Problem

Lack of convenience when shopping for thrifted items.

Americans' homes are filled w/ clothes that are unused, or no longer suited to their personal taste. Users don’t have the time or ability to visit physical stores and would rather buy their clothes on the go or from home from trusted sellers.

The Solution

Building a unique, reliable experience for consumers.

Nifty Thrifty has:

  • A recommended for you page tailored to the users' likes
  • The ability to read a seller’s reviews from previous comments
  • Keep up with trusted sellers by following their stories on the shopping page

Competitive Analysis of Mercari and Poshmark

Visually appealing, with slight misses.

To begin my research, I conducted a competitive analysis of Mercari and Poshmark, two very popular thrifting apps. Both had strong branding and friendly language, yet Poshmark's UI seemed a bit cluttered and overloaded with features, while locating purchase history on Mercari was difficult.

User Testing: Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Users were able to navigate quickly and efficiently.

My biggest goal with this usability test was to measure how easy it was for my users to perform a task that was fundamental to their shopping experience. This also helps to ensure that my design relies on Nifty Thrifty's values, which are convenience and efficiency.

Number of Testers: 3

Task:

Navigate from onboarding screens to item of clothing page and add to cart.

The Main Insight

Users want more interactivity between the shopper and seller.

Based on my users' feedback, users found the app easy to navigate and aesthetically pleasing. However, users wanted more social interaction between the seller and customer.
